About Me

I am a fourth-year student at the University of Michigan, graduating in Fall 2025 with a double major in English and Computer Science. I am passionate about building technology that is thoughtful, accessible, and impactful.

My academic work spans programming, data structures and algorithms, web systems, user interface development, and computer organization. I enjoy applying my skills to projects that blend technical rigor with creativity, and I am excited to continue learning and growing.

Coursework at The University of Michigan

EECS 485: Web Systems

Project-based course practicing the use of HTML, CSS, Python, JavaScript, etc. Course material prioritizes learning both front and back end web system technologies. The first half of the course features an Instagram-esque project, and the second half has a search engine project. Click here for more information on this course!

EECS 493: User Interface development

Course centered around the development and iterative improvement of a team project. Project focuses user-centered design, prototyping, and research. Course materials work with HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and more. Click here for more information on this course!

EECS 370: Intro to Computer Orgnanization

Project-based course teaching the back-end actions of computers and how computers interact with low-level languages. The course works primarily with the language C when needed.

EECS 376: Foundations of Computer Science

Assignment-based course on Computer Science theory. Many of the course's topics seek to analyze complex algorithms and to explain why those algorithms are complex. Click here for more information on this course!

EECS 281: Data Structures and Algorithms

Project-based course covering fundamental algorithms and their uses. The course also focuses heavily on the optimization of programs, for both memory and runtime. The course uses C++ exclusively. Click here for more information on this course!

Other Work

She's the First at Michigan Winter 2024 Newsletter

I was the Global Awareness Project (GAP) Committee Chair for She's the First (STF) at Michigan, the University of Michigan's student organized chapter of the larger organization She's the First, for the 2023-2024 academic year. As GAP chair, I put together two semester newsletters featuring articles written by some of the club's members along with the occasional art pieces by some of our members. I used the occasional Canva template as a base, but about half of the pages are my own work. Pages two through five are my own designs, and the rest of the pages alternate between my own designs and designs that used Canva templates that I modified (to varying degrees). Please note that pages 15-17 are an art piece by another STF at UMich member and are not my own work.
